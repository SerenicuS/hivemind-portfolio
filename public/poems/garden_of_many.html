The Garden

We are forced to live without our opinion,
Suffered and harmed by our situation.
We did not choose this, yet we are being punished,
Trying to understand why we wished.
Can I just die and forget about all of this existence?
So you can sleep peacefully, with innocence.

You found the garden is barren, when you arrived in this world.
Perhaps you could plant flowers,
Like the stories that parents read to their children,
Creating warm memories that last a while.

You died without seeing your garden bloom,
Flowers flourishing, with boons.
Your son found the same garden, with the same burden,
Asking questions of your garden, which you viewed as treasure.
Why does the garden look ordinary?

They do not start from scratch.
The garden was shaped by your mother.
The flowers were planted by you, his father.
Why do we cling to older traditions, thinking the garden should stay barren?
Instead of planting something that our children can thrive upon?

Let us try to make the world greener,
And fulfill the hope we wished to see.
